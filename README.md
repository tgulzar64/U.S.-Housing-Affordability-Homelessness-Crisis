# U.S. Housing Affordability & Homelessness Crisis  
### A Multi-City, Data-Driven Analysis (Boston, New York, Chicago, Los Angeles, Washington D.C.)

This project investigates the **growing housing affordability crisis in the United States** and its implications for **housing insecurity and homelessness**. Using housing price indices, wage data, renter affordability metrics, and time-series forecasting models, the analysis evaluates whether current housing market trends are economically sustainable for middle- and lower-income households.

The study combines **economic analysis, forecasting, and policy-oriented insights** to assess how rising housing costs are structurally excluding large segments of the population from both homeownership and renting.
